Schoolboy rugby enthusiasts are excited about what is next in store for the new schoolboy rugby year and the Damelin Night Series hosted by Port Natal in Kwazulu-Natal is already in full swing. Now it’s time to have a look at the schools with the best squads and top class brain trusts working behind the scenes in pre-season preparations.

Schoolboy rugby coaching can be a very popular occupation. Let’s not forget a coach’s role is to plan, control, implement and sustain schoolboy rugby structures by helping to nurture the next future Springbok stars for South Africa.

Personally I’ve always acknowledged that I’m a huge admirer of Grey College. Looking at the amount of world class Springboks produced and overall talent developed, most schools in South Africa struggle to come close to the “rugby factory”. By their own high standards set in previous years, 2012 was an unsuccessful season for the boys from Bloemfontein. Looking at the results from their own junior teams last year, no doubt the 1st team is sure to bounce back in style again. Forwards coach Ingo Machts expects a well drilled pack led by their highly rated props Andrew du Plessis, Ruan Kramer, and Reuben Terblanche. Grade 11 pupils Alexander Jonker and Lamond Bouwer will fight it out for a spot in the loose trio alongside incumbents Marius Louw and Reinhardt Arndt. The backline coach Roelf Meyer is expected to unleash centres Julian Jordaan, Carel Durow, and the versatile EW Viljoen; one can expect a potent backline this year. It’s worth noting that former Springbok visual coach Sherylle Calder is also involved with the 1s team with an office situated by the gym.

It’s going to take some effort to unseat Affies as the powerhouse of schoolboy rugby in Gauteng. The men from Pretoria are led by a coaching set up consisting of Peregrine Joynt and Heinrich van Jaarsveldt. The man behind their recent success is 1st team head coach Sakkie van der Walt, who has been running the ship for more than a decade.

The “Die Wit Bulle” team has five influential players to build the team around this year. Again their massive tight five is expected to provide a solid platform for the backs to work on. Up front prop WP Eloff will look to establish his reputation as a force to be reckoned with in the set pieces. Nuggetty hooker and 2012 Bulls Craven Week representative Francois Steyn will cause a few headaches for opponents with his speed. Not many sides will fancy playing against the highly rated lock, Rudolf G. Snyman, either. The towering lock has a bright future ahead, a sure bet to make SA Schools this year.

Number 8 Calvon Allison will play a major role as a ball carrier and a link between the backs and forwards. 2012 SA Schools centre Jurie Linde will be a thorn for many sides with his pace and game-breaking abilities. Also, the versatile Eduard Keyter is a very dangerous runner in open play with ball in hand.

Like last season, Paarl Gym are expected to rattle a few feathers. Under the guidance of the highly rated 1st team coach Christoph Lotter and his right hand-man Hendrik Weber, the boys from the Western Cape will prove to be a daunting prospect for most teams. The side boasts three potential 2013 SA Schools players in the form of imposing 8th man Rikus Bothma and flank Jacques Vermuelen. The promising lock JD Schikerling is a rising star having already represented the SA Schools Side in 2012. Though he’s not the biggest player on the field, right wing and WP Craven Week representative Grant Hermanus has speed to burn out wide. Ryan Oosthuizen, a member of the 2013 SA schools sevens side that won gold at the Australian Youth Olympics, is expected to pull the strings in midfield. Interestingly, many people have questioned how the team will cope without the current Baby Boks flyhalf Handre Pollard.

Neighbours Paarl Boys High will have relatively new faces coming into the team this year. 1st team coach Elmo Wolfaardt and his assistants Pierre Ribbens and Gerrie Visser will run the show from the side-lines this season. There’s no doubt that the Boishaai’s running game and impressive skill set will thrill many spectators across the country. Four usual suspects are expected to make a welcome return to the side but big tighthead prop Thomas du Toit will need to come out of his shell and provide some grunt up front.

Whether he’s playing lock or loose forward, make no doubt about it, Burger van Niekerk is a player worth keeping an eye on. The fleet-footed Dewald Nuade is an exciting player at centre with ball in hand. On attack the frail appearing wing Dante van der Merwe could be a very useful player coming from full back.

Paul Roos have gone through some changes in their coaching set up for this season. It’s well known that Dawie Snyman (snr), uncle of the WP junior coach Dawie Snyman (jnr), has been appointed as the new skills coach. The Maroon machine will be glad to have Hein Kriek on board as head coach. Having coached two Under15A unbeaten teams in the last three years at two different schools, he comes highly recommended.

From what I’ve been told, Paul Roos have a young team with a potent backline this year. It’s no secret that the extremely talented Daniel du Plessis, son of Springbok legend Michael Du Plessis, is their key playmaker. It might just be a very good year for junior maties.

Boland Landbou are more than capable of pulling a few shocks against the more fancied Boishaai, Paarl Gym, and Paul Roos. I expect the farmers to have a very good year under head coach Eloff Bergh and assistant Hottie Louw, a former WP lock. Tian Nel will be expected to dictate play with his educated big boot at flyhalf. Also, fullback Heinrich Buhr is one of the key players to watch in a very young and exciting backline. Opponents will know after the match who prop Matthys Basson is as this big unit will be a very influential player in the tight five.

On paper it’s looking like Outeniqua High School may very well be the team to beat in the cape. The men from South Western Districts know how to keep us entertained with their exciting attacking rugby. Deputy Head of Sport Pieter Cloete will be joined in the coaching set up by Martin Oosthuizen and Jacques Wolfaardt.

The Kwaggas tight five is going to raise a few eyebrows as most of the schoolboy players have represented SWD at junior and senior level. Tighthead Prop Wynand de Neeker and hooker Marne Botha will lead the charge up front supported by the talented SA Under 16 high Performance member Eduard Zandberg at lock. Dian Koen is a good ball carrier at Number 8 with Lian Schalkwyk and George Malan at flank. All three loose forwards form a very powerful back row behind the scrum. The backline is almost littered with game breakers in the shape of their exciting half back pairing, the versatile Remu Malan and nippy flyhalf Dewald Human. It’s no secret that 2012 SA Schools representatives wing Duhan van der Merwe and the multi-talented Warrick Gelant are both key players in the team. Don’t be surprised if Outeniqua have one of their best seasons in years.

Just across the west rand in Johannesburg, Krugersdorp’s Monument High School will look to return to their glory days under new Director of Rugby Hans Coetzee. His experience while working at the Lions Rugby Unions with the senior side and overseeing junior structures for the pride of Jozi will prove invaluable. Monument will not have many star names but a few players have impressed over the past year, especially powerful prop Frans Van Wyk who is a very good ball carrier and is very solid in the scrums. Their pacey 8th man, Wiehan Jacobs, is a very strong player and the versatile Christoff Venter is a very talented player, definitely an asset for the backline. Not many teams will want to face Hans Coetzee’s boys as he has a great record as a coach at Monnas.

Of the Pretoria sides, Waterkloof High School can never ever be written off. Last year they surprisingly beat arch rivals Affies in a classic clashes derby. There were a few question marks over Springbok legend Naas Botha joining the coaching ranks at kloffies, but so far it has been a smooth learning curve for the Bulls legend who is joined on the bench by 1st XV head coach Jan Mollentze and Burger Lindeque.

One can always expect a mobile forward pack from Kloffies, and highly rated props Xander van Wyk and Johan van Wyk will be more than confident in matching just about anyone in the scrums. Their loose trio consists of the very talented brothers David and Sean Joyce, who will provide a physical presence at the flanks, and skilful 8th man Nardus Erasmus who is a highly skilled player. It comes as no surprise the key player in the backline will be scrumhalf and 2012 SA Schools member, Justin Phillips, who’ll dictate matters in open play.

There is no denying that Glenwood High School is the team to beat again this year in KwaZulu-Natal. Looking at their recent success, the Green Machine has been clearly dominating schoolboy rugby in those parts for the past several years. Head coach Sean Erasmus has made an outstanding contribution to the 1st team and is heavily backed by the school’s excellent coaching staff, like assistant coach Rudi Dames, and some very sound talent identification structures. The Director of Rugby has gained invaluable knowledge and input from working at the Sharks Academy with the likes of Sean Everitt, current Sharks head coach John Plumtree, and Rudolph Strauaeli. It is expected that Glenwood will have another useful side again and key for them unlocking most defences will be the side’s potently quick backline. A slight concern is that the forwards may struggle to win the physical exchanges against schools with bigger packs of forwards. Personally, I’m most keen to watch young Morné Joubert. The elusive, skilful, quick, and versatile player looks certain to be a potential future star in the making.
