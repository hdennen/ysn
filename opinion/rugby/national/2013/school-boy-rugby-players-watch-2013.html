The schoolboy rugby season is officially underway with Port Natal in Umbilo showcasing some of the best talent in the Damelin Night Series from lesser known schools in Kwazulu Natal. When it comes to pre-season discussions, one question that’s on everyone’s lips is “Who’s the most talented player of this year?"

There were plenty of players who stepped into the spotlight last year, most notably the SA schools scrumhalf Justin Phillips, possibly one of the most exciting players at schoolboy rugby level.

During the 2012 Craven Week held in Port Elizabeth he rattled a few feathers with his uncanny ability to display game breaking abilities reminiscent of Springbok and Bulls legend Joost van der Westhuizen. With his tactical acumen and try scoring instincts opponents will have to keep a very close eye on him. Without a doubt he’s a massive loss for the Blue Bulls Rugby Union as it’s widely known he has committed his future to WP after completing his studies at Waterkloof High School. Elsewhere anchoring the scrum for Kloffies will be tighthead prop Johan van Wyk who caught the eye last season while playing for the for Blue Bulls Academy Week side. On the other side of the scrum he’ll be joined by the equally promising loosehead prop Xander van Wyk. These two highly rated players will be expected to make a massive contribution to the Kloffies tight five with their high work rate, powerful ball carrying abilities, and set-piece dominance up front.

Just next door to loftus, Affies will look to their pacy 2012 SA Schools outside centre Jurie Linde to make another massive impact in the backline. The influential player has an eye for a gap, is deadly with ball in hand, and is solid on defence.Nuggety hooker Francois Steyn who possesses great speed for a forward will look to continue his superb form which earned him a Bulls Craven Week call up last year. For all the talented lock forwards on display last season none were more impressive than the towering lock, young Rudolph G. Snyman, who was voted the best forward in the 2012 Kearsney Easter Rugby Festival festival. Sadly the lanky lock forward suffered a double leg break thus ruling him out of Craven Week and SA Schools contention. Now that he's fully fit and raring to go, don’t be surprised if this player causes a stir amongst rugby enthusiasts in the schoolboy rugby scene. After all this highly promising player may just be the real deal.

Something special is brewing for the 2012 Atlantic Schools Champions Garsfontein High School in Pretoria. A host of new exciting talent has emerged this year led by 2012 Bulls Craven Week representative and lock forward Hyron Andrews. Another player worth keeping an eye on is number 8 Ruan Ackerman, son of former Springbok lock and current Lions coach Johan Ackerman. If anything, he goes like his father - the young loose forward is tenacious, solid defensively, and a superb ball carrier in open play. Wing Marquit September, a member of the Bulls Craven Week side in Port Elizabeth, sure knows his way to the try line. The fleet-footed player is always looking for work and he doesn’t shy away from his defensive responsibility, definitely a key player in the ”Garsies” backline.

Another schoolboy player worth keeping tabs on is young Vean Roodt from Centurion High School. The aggressive hooker is solid in the scrums and very useful around the park. His game resembles that of Stormers and 2012 WP Currie Cup-winning captain Deon Fourie. Southdowns College have a potential star in the making in grade 11 pupil, Aston Fortuin. Regardless of the controversy surrounding his move from Queens College, this young exciting lock is a potential star in the making.

As we make our way down to Bloemfontein and into the ”rugby factory”, Grey College boast four quality schoolboy players returning from last year’s 1ST team. Their 2012 Craven Week tight head prop Ruan Kramer will look to continue his superb form up front for the tight five. The promising open side flank Marius Louw is built in the mould of Heinrich Brussow and was also part of the South African u18 Sevens team that represented South Africa at the Australian Olympic Youth Festival. Supporting him behind the scrum will be the immensely talented Number 8, Reinhardt Arndt, who’ll be an important link between the backs and forwards. There is no doubt about it, their go-to man in the backline will be the versatile EW Viljoen, a member of the 2013 SA Sevens u18 side. He is definitely one of the hottest properties in schoolboy rugby.

Situated in the small town of Botha’s Hill in KwaZulu-Natal, Kearsney College will look the outstanding brothers Jean-Luc Du Preez and Daniel Du Preez to make another massive impact in 2013. It came to no surprise at all when both brothers were chosen for the SA Schools team in 2012. The twins were so influential that both were above their peers in terms of the level of consistency and startling performance shown on the field. One can clearly see that they are physically gifted, well-conditioned, great ball carriers, and most importantly they provide a physical presence up front. An interesting development will see Daniel make a positional switch from lock to blindside flank. Impressive Jean-Luc is already proving to be quiet a thorn at open side flank, and together with his brother behind the scrum will be more than a handful for opponents.

One player expected to make a big impression for Glenwood High School is the Pretoria born and lightning quick Morné Jourbert. He’s very well-balanced with ball in hand and in full flight a sight to behold - no doubt his versatility will prove invaluable.

In the Eastern Cape it’s no secret that Framesby’s Ernst Stapelberg is probably one of the best flyhalves in the country. The tactically sound pivot has a massive boot on him, very dependable on defence, and most importantly he knows how to control a game. he’s an exciting prospect for the future. It also remains to be seen how the talented Peet Schoeman, a member of the 2011 SA Under 16 High Performance Squad goes at centre for Marlow Agricultural High School.

Outeniqua High School’s wonderfully gifted players, the versatile Warrick”Boogie” Gelant and Duhan van der Merwe, were part of a triumphant 2013 SA Sevens Under-18 team that won Gold at the Australian Youth Olympics. They certainly have a bright future ahead for themselves. Kwaggas from the South-Western-Districts region will look to their multi-talented ringmasters to provide a spark to the backline with their flair, skill, and speed. Both exciting players will prove to be a thorn for most sides that come up against them. Not far behind them though is their influential half back pairing consisting of the versatile Remu Malan running the show at scrumhalf and the exciting 2013 SA Sevens Under-18 member Dewald Human at pivot.

There’s no denying that Western Province has more quality schoolboy rugby players than any other province and 2013 will be no different as most opponents will not enjoy having to come up against Boland Landbou monster tight head prop Matthys Basson.

As expected Paul Roos will always be a quality outfit and in Daniel du Plessis, son of ex-Springbok Michael du Plessis, they have an exciting prospect. A huge worry though is that the young playmaker may not be ready to play due to a long term injury he picked up last year. When fully fit he could be key in the backline for the maroon machine from Stellenbosch.

Not a lot is known about Paarl Boys High School this year but they will have many new faces compared to last year’s impressive side. In Burger van Niekerk they have an outstanding player with a massive future ahead of him. The tall athletic lock and loose forward player is a very good ball carrier and very technically sound in the line outs.

Paarl Gimnasium looks set to have another great season ahead for them and 2012 SA Schools lock JD Schickerling is certainly a rising star for the future. ”Jakkals” showed some extraordinary line out work and great athleticism around the field in 2012; he’s definitely going to prove to be a daunting task for most teams this season. Their star Number 8 Rikus Bothma has been tipped to be one of the stars for this year’s schoolboy rugby season. The highly rated loose forward is a powerful ball carrier and an inspirational leader in the pack. Let’s not forget about Jacques Vermuelen, this defensively sound blindside flank is definitely a contender for SA Schools selection this year.

Hanro Liebenberg, younger brother of 2012 Baby Boks Captain Wiaan Liebenberg, certainly is a name worth remembering. Many schoolboy rugby followers have not heard about the 1.95 metre giant 8th man from HTS Drostdy. Mark my words he’s a potential star in the making. All that can be said is watch this space!

Without a doubt not many of these players below in the list will be the next Jan Serfontein or Eben Etzebeth. What they possess though is exceptional talent and superstar potential to become household names in South African rugby.

However future success will be only be rewarded, determined, and achieved by those who knuckle down and apply themselves on and off the field. A lot of these schoolboy rugby players have a great opportunity to follow in the footsteps of 2012 Craven Week Player of the year and EP Kings speedster Sergeal Petersen.
