Yaseen Valli

School KES
Province Gauteng
High Achievment SA U19
Position All Rounder

The KES all-rounder is set to be the selector’s first name on the team sheet for the SA schools squad that will be selected at the tournament's conclusion. Valli has been scoring buckets of runs for Old Eds along with team mate Grant Roelofsen. The SA U19 captain will look to rectify his exclusion in last year's SA schools XI after he only managed to make the SA Colts XI. The talented all-rounder looks to continue his impressive form.

Dayyaan Galiem

School Rondebosch
Province WP
High Achievment SA U19
Position All Rounder

One of WP’s youngest U19 campaigners, Dayyaan Galiem, is one of the brightest and most promising talents in the country. The rising start hasn’t looked back since breaking into the Rondebosch 1st team at an early age. Galiem has since gone on to represent the SA U19, WP U19 – all of this at the age of 16. The natural athlete is gifted with ball as much as with bat and has been dubbed the next Jacques Kallis. He is sure to impress again at this year’s Coke Week.

Bradley Dial

School St Johns
Province Gauteng
High Achievment SA u19
Position Batsmen

St John’s Bradley Dial has the ability to single-handedly win a game for his team with his aggressive big hits and batting approach. Dial proved this with seven 6’s and 170 runs at last year’s coke week.

Kagiso Radaba

School St Stithians
Province Gauteng
High Achievment SA U19
Position Bowler

Radaba formed part of the strong Gauteng team at last year's week and earned an SA Colts XI selection. Kagiso played a great role in the SA U19's quadrangular tour in India. A strong quick bowler you gives his team the extra edge with the bowling attakck. Ray Jennings has described him as a unique talent not found often in South Africa as he has a raw ability with the bat down the order to go with his striker bowling. Radaba has also been fast tracked to train with the Highveld Lions by Coach Toyana. Radaba already has a semi-pro contract with the Gauteng Strikers.

Andile Phehlukawayo

School Glenwood
Province KZN Coastal
High Achievment SA U19
Position Bowler

One to keep a close eye on, Andile Phehlukwayo is the Glenwood High School and KZN Coastal fast bowler who’s built like a rugby lock. He will look to cause problems for batsmen with his pace and bounce of the ball. The grade 11 boys who broke into this year’s SA U19 that competed at the Quadrangular Series in India might be amongst the favourites for a spot in the SA Schools XI. Andile has also been scoring runs for African Warriors club in KZN Premier league.

Sibonelo Makhanya

School DHS
Province KZN Coastal
High Achievment SA U19
Position All-rounder

The DHS bowler had a fairly successful Coke Week last year as he was KZN Coastal's top wicket taker with 11 wickets. Although failing to make the SA Schools XI last year, his performance in this year’s SA U19 team will sure make him a solid contender for this competitive position. Although some might feel he under performed at last years Coke Week with the bat. He'll be hoping to bring in his form with the bat from last years u19 3-day comp for KZN where he scored a hundred against Free State.

Greg Oldfield

School Michaelhouse
Province KZN-Inland
High Achievment SA U19
Position Batsmen

One of the most talented and consistent batsmen in school boy cricket, Greg Oldfield will be looking to improve his already impressive stats. Greg finished 3rd in top run makers in last year’s Coke Week with 278 runs in 7 innings and averaging 46.33 runs. Away from school cricket, Oldfield, has turned out for Pinetown CC and has earned a semi-pro contract for KZN next season. The Michaelhouse batsmen has earned the title of a bowlers nightmare.

Clyde Fortuin

School St Josephs
Province WP
High Achievment SA U19
Position Batsmen

WP Batsmen Clyde Fortuin rewards coach Ray Jennings for the faith he showed in selecting the 2012 SA Colts player in this year's SA U19 squad with some spectacular batting and wicket keeping performances. Clyde Fortuin has been scoring runs regularly for WPCC in the WP mens Premier league over the last 2 seasons,  will look to carry this form over to this year's coke week.

Ngazibini Sigwili

School Hudson Park
Province Border
High Achievment SA U19
Position Bowler

The Border skiddy left-arm swing bowler was a main feature in the SA U19 XI this year and formed a very lethal bowling partnership with Kagiso Radaba. His stellar performance for the SA U19 side came as no surprise as the bowler had a very successful performance for Border in last year’s Khaya Majola Cricket Week - taking 13 wickets to finish off as the 7th top wicket taker. Sigwili was also the leading wicket taker in the u19 limited overs series against England at the beginning of the year.

Leus Du Plooy

School Affies
Province Northerns
High Achievment U19 Camp
Position Batsman

A left-handed top order batsman who prefers the number 3 role as opposed to opening like he did at the last Coke Week, Du Plooy is a good leader and has been part of the Affies first team since the end of his Grade 8 year. Du Plooy recently scored a hundred for CBC Old Boys in the strong Northerns Premier Club League. Leus is a tight and compact batsman who likes to spend time at the crease and is capable of getting big scores for his team. A very handy left-arm spinner as well, Leus also formed part of the CSA u19 camp.

Lloyd Brown

School Grey High
Province EP
High Achievment SA U19
Position Batsman

Lloyd will be leading the EP Coke week team for the second year running. He is a right-handed batsman, capable of batting in the top to middle order. He doesn't have the prettiest of techniques but has very good hand-eye co-ordination. Lloyd was also part of the SA u19 squad that toured India.

Zubayr Hamza

School Rondebosch
Province WP
High Achievment N/A
Position Batsman

Hamza is a very good right-handed middle order batsman who will lead WP at this year’s Coke Week. Hamza has very good wrists which enable him to manipulate the ball very well, but he is also capable of finding the boundary. Unfortunately due to his hockey commitments he was unable to attend the CSA u19 camp and couldn't earn SA u19 selection.

Ryan Rickelton

School St Stithians
Province Gauteng
High Achievment U19 Camp
Position Batsman

Rickelton will be appearing in his first Coke Week. Ryan also scooped the CSA u15 player of the year for 2011/12 season. He is a left-handed opening batsman who can keep wicket, as well as an adaptable batsman as he can score runs from the T20 format through to time cricket where he gets hundreds. In the first term of 2013 he got 5 hundreds for Saints 1st XI including a double century vs Centurion in a 2-day game then he scored a hundred in the Jonny Waite T20 final. Then subsequently went on to have a good School Coke T20 Tournament where he got two 50's with a more than impressive strike-rate. Ryan was also part of the CSA u19 and CSA u17 training camps in recent months.

Jurie Snyman

School Jeugland
Province Gauteng
High Achievment U19 Camp
Position Batsman

Jurie Snyman is a beefy left-handed middle-order batsman who will be attending his third Coke Week. He was the CSA u15 cricketer of the season for 2009/10 & 2010/11 due to his good ball-striking ability and regular run scoring at youth level for Easterns and Jeugland’s 1st XI. Snyman has been involved with the Easterns Provincial cricket team this season where he has scored 3 fifties in 4 innings in the 50-over format. Snyman scored a hundred at last year’s Coke Week.

Grant Roelofsen

School KES
Province Gauteng
High Achievment N/A
Position Batsman

Roelofsen is a right-handed top order batsman who will be attending his first Coke Week. He is currently in a rich vein of form. Grant has scored 3 or 4 hundreds for the KES 1st XI and is also leading the averages in Gauteng Premier Club cricket for Old Edwardians.

Sohail Mahmoud

School Maritzburg College
Province KZN Inland
High Achievment SA Colts XI
Position Bowler

Sohail is a right-arm leg-spinner. He made the SA Colts last year and has another 2 years to go and might be in the mix for the next 2 upcoming Coke Week's. Mahmoud definitely has ability and he can bat too. Sohail was definitely the best spinner at last year’s Coke Week even at 16 and was unlucky to not make the SA schools side.
