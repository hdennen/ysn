Future PSL Players on Display on Day 2 of the Sanlam Kay Motsepe Schools Cup

By: 
Lungelo Mdladla
Tue, 07/10/2014 - 22:10
  • Dillan Fransch
Sibusiso Skhosana
Dillan Fransch
Clinton Herwell

Top SA Schools soccer is currently on show at the Giant Stadium in Soshanguve as 18 top teams from around SA battle it out for bragging rights and a cool one million rand.

The boys from St Marks must have caused the latest upset of the tournament after they beat a quality Holy Trinity squad. Holy Trinity held most of the possession throughout the match but a counter attack goal from St Marks saw them take a 1 - 0 lead just before half time.

From then on centre back and St Marks captain Sibusiso Skhosana marshalled his defenders as they halted all Holy Trinity attempts at goal. Skhosana reminded his players to attack and to play as if the score is 1 - 1. Pushing his team to not lose focus.

 
Post by Youth Sports Network.

St Marks held on much to their jubilation. Credit must go to both teams for a quality match.

 
Post by Youth Sports Network.

 

 
Post by Youth Sports Network.

The boys from KZN, Tholukuhle have held their own and so far haven't lost a game in this year's tournament. However, in the last round of today's matches they were pushed to the wire by a strong Sebetwana team from the North West. Tholukuhle scored an equalizer late in the 2nd half after the ball fell nicely for their goal scorer in the box. The match ended 1 - 1.

 
Post by Youth Sports Network.

This tournament sees tons of SA representatives and I'll be sure to let you know which schools they come from tomorrow. The Orlando Pirates Academy boys from Thaba Jabula also held Holy Trinity to a draw.

 
Post by Youth Sports Network.

The boys from the Western Cape Mondale have some pure talent with the likes Dillan Fransch and Clinton Herwel. Mondale haven't lost a game and topped their pool with 14 points that include 4 wins and 2 draws. We'll also hear from scouts on their thoughts on the tournament.
