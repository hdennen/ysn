Hoer Landbou Oakdale vs Sharks Academy

Landbou Oakdale and Sharks Academy broke the ice on the second day of the Wildeklawer Superskole. It took 3 minutes for Oaksdale to find their first try with Juan Terblanche going over the line, converted by JT Jackson. 5 Minutes later Terblanche scored his second try in a game characterized by one-way traffic from Oakdale. They managed to score 4 more tries, including one for Jackson who finished the game with 15 points for his side. Sharks Academy managed a solitary penalty to get on the scoreboard although Boland ended with a very convincing win over the Sharks Academy.

Waterkloof vs Paarl Gim

The second game was a tantalizing propect between Paarl Gim and Waterkloof. Watekloof suffered a defeat against Outeniqua the previous day unlike Paarl Gim who beat Wildeklawer Invitational (Hentie Ciliers) 43-18. After a tentative opening period, Grant Hermanus went over the try line for Paarl Gim's first try.  Waterkloof responded 2 minutes later through the boot of Tinus de Beer, and 9 Minutes later, Waterkloof got another penalty allowing Tinus de Beer to put his side ahead by a point. From the 21st minute, after a Ryan Oostuizen try, it was all Paarl Gim. Waterkloof did not add to their score for the remainder of the match while Paarl Gim added 29 more points through the boot of Wyatt Murphy and tries by Brendan Nell, Rikus Bothma, and Dylan Fairbridge. Paarl crushed Waterkloof 34-6.

Diamantveld vs EG Jansen

EG Jansen demolished Diamantveld 63-0 in the most one-sided affair at the Wildeklawer Superskole. EG Jansen led 21-0 at half time before the flood gates opened. 43 points were scored in the second half by the “Jannies” men, rocketing the side to the top of the festival points log.

Grey High vs Outeniqua

Outeniqua started the match well, finding the try line in the 5th minute though the ever consistent Leighton Eksteen, who converted his own try. Grey High hit back almost immediately with Luca Dellavechia going over in the 8th minute for Grey High's first try. Richard Bryant made the scores level with his conversion. 2 minutes later Eduard Zandberg restored Oueniquas lead with a 5 pointer.  A penalty was awarded to Grey High in the 16th minute cutting Outeniqua's lead down to 2 points. The prolific Duhan van der Merwe scored his 3rd try of the festival in the 22nd minute for Outeniqua and Warrick Gelant gave Outeniqua a bit more breathing room after he scored a try converted by Eksteen. Outeniqua went into half time with a 22-10 lead. Shortly after the whistle signaled the start of the second half, Richard Bryant scored a try to reduce the deficeit to 7 points. Outeniqua found another gear after the Bryant try and went on to score 4 more tries, 2 by Geor Malan, one from Marne Both and Duhan van der Merve with the final try of the game. After an early exchange, Outeniqua proved their superiority winning 48-15 against the men from the Eastern Cape.

Glenwood vs Paarl Boys High

In what was arguably the game of the day and tournament, Glenwood clinched a very narrow 17-15 win over Paarl Boys High. Paarl Boys High had the better of the 1st half with Ferlin Vers scoring first for Paarl, putting 5 points on the board with Damian Stevens converting. Damian Stevens put his side further ahead when he slotted over a penalty to give Paarl a 10 point lead going into half time. The score was 10-0 to Paarl Boys. In what was to be a game of two halves, Glenwood responded well after the half time break. Nkululeko Mcuma started the scoring when he went over for Glenwood's first try in the 34th minute. Corne Vermaak converted the try to leave his team 3 points behind. Paarl Boys hit back after Glenwood's half time revival when Thomas du Toit scored his side's 2nd try to extend the points gap to 8 points. Then a 45th minute try by Kevin du Randt, converted by Vermaak, brought the men from Glenwood within a single point. With all eyes on him, Vermaak fired a 45 metre penalty in the 55th minute, all but securing Glenwood's 17-15 victory over Paarl Gim.

Wildeklawer(Hentie Ciliers) vs HTS Drostdy

Hentie Ciliers will rue not making the most of their possession and superior lineout after losing 29-7 to HTS Drosdy. Drosdty were more clinical and used “the pill” more efficiently compared to their opponents. HTS Drostdy dominated the early proceeding, which continued for most of the game. Corne Nel scored two tries in the opening 12 minutes for Drostdy. Wynand Roets managed to convert one of them to put his 12-0 up. Selwin White and Ziaan Barnard scored more tries in the second half, further stretching their lead. Hentie finally got on the scoreboard in the 50th minute when Ethan Williams scored his side first and only try, converting it himself. However, Drostdy had the final say as Tiaan Muller scored another try to cap his side’s dominant performance.

Boland Landbou vs Noord-Kaap

Boland Landbou and Noord-Kaap played to a close end, Boland managing to edge the match by 4 points over Noord-Kaap. The two sides kept trading spells of superiority thoughout the match. A penalty by Tian Nel and try by Riaan Sutherland, converted by Nel gave Boland a 10-0 in the opening 10 minutes. 2 penalties by Dimitrio Tieties cut the deficit to 4 points before half time. A try shortly after half time by Luan Bantjies and converted by Nel extended the side's advantage. A penalty slotted by Nel put his side further ahead. Noord-Kaap looked to mount a comeback after Hilford Clarke scored 5 points, converted by Tieties to bring the scores closer. A 50th minute penalty by Tieties brought his side within 4 points of Boland. The Boland men held on for a 20-16 win.

Affies vs Paul Roos

In a game of two half’s, Affies came from 10 down to win the match 17-10. Paul Roos started the better of the two teams, scoring all the points in the first half. A try by Mitchell Casten, converted by Aiden Cupido drew first blood. Another Cupido penalty added 3 more points to the Paul Roos score. The rest of the half remained quiet, neither side adding any points to the scoreboard. Affies came out and showed why they haven’t lost a single game since the rugby season began mounting a comeback through SA U18 Jurie Linde. A conversion by Ivan van Zyl brought the deficit down to 3 points. Another try by Affies through DP de Plessis gave “die Wit Bulle” the lead for the first time in the match. A van Zyl conversion extended the lead by 2 points. A 49th minute penalty by van Zyl put some daylight between Affies and Paul Roos. Paul Roos were unable to score any points in the second half capping a great Affies comeback and making sure they remained unbeaten.

Grey College vs Hoerskool Monument

In a low scoring weather affected match, Grey College managed to pip monument 13-8. Despite not being able to play to their usual style of running rugby, Grey High proved that they are still a force to be reckoned with regardless of the conditions. Both sides scored a try each, the difference made by the boot. Two early 2nd half penalties from Stephen Rautenbach was enough to secure the victory for his team.

Frameby vs Maritzburg

The last game of Wildeklawer Superskole was an exciting back and forth between the hosts and Framesby. Framesby were first to put points on their scoreboard after Ernst Stapelberg dotted down in the 12th minute for Framesby's first try. He converted his own try. Maritzburg with the majority of the possession managed to find a break through in the 26th minute, Lindo Ngcobo going over for 5 points. 2 minutes later College were at it again, this time Man of the Match Simon Culverwell securing the 5 points for College. With 68% of the possession, College had a narrow 3 point lead at half time. Stapelberg wiped off College's lead after slotting a penalty in the 40th minute to draw his side level. Marcel Coetzee responded for Maritzburg, taking back the lead with a 3 pointer. Stapelberg leveled up matters again, slotting over another penalty in the 54th minute. With 3 minutes remaining, College got another penalty which was converted by Coetzee for a narrow 3 point lead. Stapelberg broke Maritzburg hearts after they were awarded a penalty in the 60th minute, and Stapelberg obliged by putting the ball between the sticks to earn his side a draw.
