After all the excitement of the Easter rugby festivals school boys' rugby teams went back to their respective home territories. Some of the finest and most exciting school rugby teams have once again assembled in Maritzburg for Wildeklawer 2013.

EG Jansen vs Noord-Kaap

EG Jansen and Noord-Kaap opened the curtain on Wildeklawer 2013 with Martin van Wyk opening up his account by scoring the first points of the festival with a try. EG Jansen dominated the opening stages and much of the match against Noord-Kaap. Noord-Kapp first points came in the 33rd minute through a try by Hilford Clarke, Dimitrio Trieties converting. EG Jansen scored 4 tries compared Noord-Kaaps 2 with Martin van Wyk scoring two of the tries with Marco Holmes and Quintin van der Shyff contributing the other. Hannes Forrester scored Noord-Kaaps second try and last points of the game, eventually falling to EG Jansen 36-12.

Glenwood vs Boland Landbou

After a rocky start, Glenwood overcame Boland Landbou by a comfortable 36-13. Boland opened the scoring through a try by Mattys Basson, converted by Tian Nel. Glenwood hit back almost immediately with a try of their own by Sphamandla Ngcobo. Two Tian Nel Penalties put some distance between the two sides before another converted try by Ngcobo brought the sides closer. It was all Glenwood from then on, Boland Landbou unable to score any points. Glenwood recovered quickly after being behind.

CLICK HERE for match breakdown and stats

HTS Drostdy vs Monument

The third game of the festival followed a similar path to the first two. Monument dominated the first half scoring all the points. 4 tries from Dwayne Pienaar, Frans van Wyk, Stefan le Roux and Willem Cloete put Monnas 27 points ahead with no reply from Drostdy. Drostdy's first point came in the 44th minute when a try by Hanro Liebenberg converted by Wynand Roets put some much-needed points on the board. Monument cancelled the 7 points acquired by Drostdy 6 minutes later after another try by Stefan le Roux, converted by Gerdus van der Walt restored the 27-point gap. Drostdy had the final say, scoring the final points of the match. A try by Yousrie Savahl, converted by Wynand Roets added some dignity to the scoreboard and the match ended 36-14 to Monnas

Affies vs Framesby

Affies took on Frameby in the 4th match of the festival. Affies have not had a loss to next to their name since the rugby season officially began. Framesby, despite showing some fight, lost to the Pretoria school 41-21. Affies had the better of the 1st half exchanges, scoring 29 points to Frameby's 6. Tries from Thys van Wyk, Jurie Linde, Eduan Keyter, and Francois Steyn ensured Affies were well comfortable going into the second half. Framesby points came off the boot of Ernst Stapelberg. Stapelberg's boot opened the scoring in the second after been given a penalty. Stapelberg was the man of the moment after he went over for his sides first try. He converted his try to bring the scores closer to 29-16. Affies responded with two tries from van Wyk and Keyter. Wessel Strydom of Framesby scored the final try of the game, ending the match 41-21.

Grey College vs Oakdale

Stephen Rautenbach opened the scoring after College earned a penalty in the 6th minute. Landbou answered almost immediately with Hanno Snyman dotting down for the first try of the game. Grey responded with a penalty to retake the lead, converted by Rautenbach. Grey took the advantage with a converted try from Henrich Sander. Grey was leading 13-6 before a try by Juan Terblanche narrowed the gap, Gene Willemse converting to level up matters at 13 all. A penalty to Oakdale allowed them to take a 3 point lead before 3 tries by Grey in the closing stages of the half by EW Viljoen, Julian Jordaan and Andries Kriek gave Grey a 30-15 lead going into the second half. 2 More tries from Grey in the second half by Marius Louw and Carel Durow put the men from Bloem further ahead.  Grey leading 42-15 had the match in their hands. A 58th minute try from Daniel Maree of Boland offered nothing more than a consolation. Grey ended a dominant display 42-20.

Sharks vs Paarl Boys High

Paarl Boys High convincingly beat the Sharks Academy 55-15. A rampant and dominant Paarl Boys High scored 8 tries their opponents 2. Despite having 82% of the ball possession in the second half, the Sharks Academy could not find holes in the Paarl defense. Paarl Boys High was clinical in their assault of the Academy try line. Daylen Daniels and Nicolas Cottee both scored a brace of tries in the demolishing of the Sharks Academy side. Only Marco de Souza and Kabelo Motluang tasted the grass on the Paarl Boys try line after dotting down for the Sharks Academy.

Diamantveld vs Grey High

Grey High dominated the 7th Game of Wildeklawer 2013. Grey annihilated Diamantveld 51-10, dominating proceeding from the first blast of the whistle - 9 tries from the Grey High boys compared to 2 from Diamantveld. Dylan Vermaak scored 2 tries straight after each in the beginning of the first half. JD Swanepoel and Marno Willemse managed to score the two tries for Diamantveld as they were outclasses by a rampant Grey High side.

CLICK HERE for match breakdown and stats

Paarl Gim vs Hentie Cilliers

Paarl Gim fresh from the win over the all-conquering Outeniqua were given a shock after falling 7-0 behind in their game against Wildeklawer (Hentie Cilliers). An 8th minute try by Sylvester Lewis, converted Ethan Williams had Paarl Gim trailing in the opening minutes of the game. Paarl Gim regrouped and showed why they beat Outeniqua, scoring 31 unanswered points before Hentie Cilliers saw their scoreboard tick over again. 6 Tries from Rikus Bothma, two from Wayno Visagie, Dylon Fairbridge, DJ van Niekerk. Wyatt Murphy converted 4 of the tries. Hentie Cilliers converted 2 penalties through Williams early in the second half. Paarl responded with 2 tries, Murphy converting the one to further stamp their authority on the match. Hentie Cilliers however had the final say with Dwayne Softley scoring a consolation try.

CLICK HERE for match breakdown and stats

Maritzburg vs Paul Roos

In one of the most eagerly awaited games of day one of Wildeklawer, Maritzburg College welcomed Paul Roos. Paul Roos drew first blood through a try from Christo van der Merwe. Paul Roos got a penalty shortly after the try for Aiden Cupido to add 3 more points. Banele Ngwenya answered for College scoring a try in the 12th minute, converted by Marcel Coetzee. Paul Roos took the initiative, scoring 17 unanswered points to put them 25-7 up. In the typical “never give up’”attitude of the College boys, two tries were scoring on either side of half time by Kelvin Elder and Ngwenya reducing the gap to 25-17. Another Paul Roos deflated the potential Maritzburg come back. Paul Roos winning the game in relative comfort 30-17.

CLICK HERE for match breakdown and stats

Waterkloof vs Outeniqua

In arguably the most anticipated game of day 1 of Wildeklawer, Waterkloof faced an Outeniqua side determined to prove that they are still top of the tree. Outeniqua dominated the first half, not allowing Waterkloof to score any points until the 46th minute. Outeniqua had the run of Waterkloof in the first half scoring 34 points. With 7 tries from Geor Malan, Duhan van der Merwe (man of the match) x2, Christopher Roelfse, Hennie Barnard, and Leighton Eksteen, Outeniqua had the game wrapped up by the 2nd half. Waterkloof however mounted a mini comeback in the second half outscoring Outeniqua in the second half. Outeniqua only added 5 more points to their first half score line through Malan again. Waterkloof scored 3 tries by Tinus de Beer x2 and David Joyce. Tinus de Beer scored 14 points, scoring and converting both of his own tries. A spirited fight back from Waterkloof was not enough to overcome the damage that had been done by Outeniqua in the first half. In a match of two very different halves, Outeniqua held on to a 39-19 victory.
