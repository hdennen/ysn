EP CD 21-11 Pumas CD

EP started the game well as they scored within two minutes of the match. Pumas managed 6 points from the boot of Rogers, who brought his team into half time trailing by 8 points. After the break Pumas scored first, cutting the deficit to 3 after Jesse du Toit’s conversion sailed wide. EP CD worked hard for their only try of the half as Kurt Ittershagen-Strauss crossed over, giving his teammates a sigh of relief while Anton Rogers provided EP with some breathing space as he comfortably converted to give his side a 10 point lead with 13 minutes to go. The Eastern Province CD team held on for their first Academy Week victory.

Free State 88-5 Limpopo Blue Bulls

Free State's 12 try haul was an example of how hard their forwards ran and the backline made use of their wings. Patrick Mbangi scored a hat trick in the first half and Stanley Ramotsana joined in with two of his own. Andre Swarts 18 points (2 tries and the rest from his boot) led his team’s 88 points tally and earned him Free State’s moment of the match award. Limpopo’s Fortune Mahasha scored first in the second half, but Free State quickly halted their opposition’s momentum as they scored 6 tries to seal the game.

Match Breakdown Here

Blue Bulls 24-20 Golden Lions

The boot of Tinus De Beer made the difference in the GP provincial teams clash. De Beer scored 14 points from his team’s 24. The Lions and Bulls were fairly matched as massive forwards were in both camps with the likes of Denzil Hill and Aston Fortuin. Flyhalf Ruan Conradie opened the scoring for the Lions before Tinus de Beer’s two penalty conversions left the Bulls a point away. Tshepo Thule took the Lions back in front before Sean Joyce responded with a try for the Blue Bulls. The only try of the match was scored in the first half as the Bulls scored just before the break, giving themselves the much needed advantage. After the break both Flyhalves scored 2 conversions apiece but the Bulls had already done enough in the first half to earn their first victory. The Lions have played some exciting rugby but have fell short by no more than 5 points, first at the hands of SWD and today the Bulls.

Match Breakdown Here

Pumas 22-14 Boland

Boland were hoping to resemble day 1’s performance of their famous win over the Blue Bulls. Boland were up 7 – 5 as both teams traded tries in the half, but the Pumas turned up the gas in the second half scoring 3 tries to Boland’s 1. Boland’s hopes were revived when Kenith Dirks scored a try in the 58th but Pumas’ Jacob Modipa made sure of victory as he scored in the 69th.

Match Breakdown Here:

SA LSEN 33-17 Griquas CD

Both teams were hungry for a win following losses on Day 1. Griquas led the half 12 – 7 as Willem Koegelenberg and Bryan van der Merwe provided the openers. LSEN pulled a try back at the end of the 1st half through Stefan van Jaarsveld, getting his team back in competition. After the break Griquas was the first to score try, but that was to be their final try and from the on is was one way traffic from LSEN who scored 4, Keanan Parnell earning a hat trick.

Match Report Here

Eastern Province 24-17 KZN

The match lived up to its anticipation as the two teams were separated by a converted try. Thabani Mgugudo continued to act as a play maker with his tactical box kicking, and Johann van Niekerk once again delivered a top drawer performance. Darren Domingo provided 11 crucial points for his EP team and kept his opposition guessing at all costs. Tristan Blewett opened the scoring for KZN inside the 10 minute mark. KZN continued the pressure when 6th man Waelon Hippolite crashed over. EP replied with a try but KZN’s Nkululeko Mcuma scored what was to be KZN’s last try. EP and KZN played a similar backline attacking game, this changed in the second half as EP looked to the boot for points and held off any of KZN’s attacks. Johann van Niekerk topped off his performance with try late into the second half.

Match Breakdown Here

Griquas 17-26 Blue Falcons

The Blue Falcons were looking to redeem themselves in today’s games after a humbling 66-5 Loss to EP in Day 1. Both sides started off positively but it was Griquas who got on the scoreboard first with a 12th minute try from Deon van Zyl.

That would be the only action on the scoreboard for the Griquas in that half as the Blue Falcons switched gears and piled the pressure on their opponents, who eventually cracked in the 27th minute with a try from Francois Dickz, which was later followed by three more tries before the end of the half, finishing off the half at 19-5 in favour of the Blue Falcons.

The Flacons picked up the 2nd half with much of the 1st half momentum which resulted in a try just 4 minutes into the 2nd half through Johannes van Tonder. Griquas tried to make a comeback and a managed to score two tries before the end of the game, but this was too little too late as the Falcons cruised to 26-17 victory, leaving the Griquas still to waiting to register a win in the tournament.

Match Breakdown Here

Griffons CD 12-55 Namibia

Our boarder neighbours continued the amazing form they have displayed so far averaging 53 points per game with a concession rate of only 6 points per game.

The Griffons set the pace of the game in the opening stages and were rewarded with a try in the 10th minute for mobile 8th man De Wet Unger, which Namibia quickly replied to with a try from tall winger Lesley Klim.

Namibia would take the remainder of the game by the scruff, as fly half Henrique Olivier speared his sides attacks and lead his side to a 55-12 victory, scoring eight tries in the process.

Match Breakdown Here

Leopards CD 0-66 KZN XV

The KZN XV side carried on their fairy tale journey from being a last minute makeshift team to replace Kenya just days before the start of the tournament, to being one of the standout performers of the tournament and undefeated.

Both sides ran onto the field looking to maintain their unbeaten recorded at the Academy Week, and KZN showed their intentions scoring an early try in the 6th minute through Ntlatla Ngubane. This would be one of many on the day as the KZN XV went on to score nine more in the game, with Maritzburg College boy Jordan Koekemoer leading the pack with 14 points.

Match Breakdown Here

Leopards  3-24 KZN CD

The opening stages of the match didn’t make for much exciting rugby as the sides had each other at a 3-3 stalemate for two thirds of the 1st half, but then the Natal boys suddenly stepped on the gas and scoreed a try through lock Reyno du Toit in the 23rd minute and finished the first half 10-3.

The Leopards looked promising in certain phases, but that pressure never materialized into anything. In the end they failed to add to their 3 points for the rest of the game to eventually go down 24-3 to a very confident KZN CD side.

Match Breakdown Here

Border 7-25 Griffons

The Griffons came onto the field running and took the game to Border from the whistle, but it was only in the 13th minute that they managed to get on the scoreboard through Johan van Zyl. It was one way traffic through the whole 1st half as Border were franticly trying to defend the Griffons without much success as the half finished 13-0 to the Griffons.

It was in the 2nd half that Border decided to arrive to the party and got their  first points on the board in the last 10 minutes of the game, but this proved a little too late as Griffons went off to win the game 25-7.

Match Breakdown Here

SWD Eagles 22-19 WP

The final and arguably most anticipated game of day 2 was between the Western Province fierce rivals. It was the boys in the blue strips who drew first blood with a try from flanker Ryan Davids, but SWD soon replied with 3 points from JT Jackson. After a lot of back and forth attacking exchange it was the Western Province who eventual gained dominance in the 1st half which ended 19-8 in their favour.

The SWD coach must have given them a much needed motivational speech as they stepped into the 2nd half the stronger side, and JT Jackson was at the thick of things as usual. SWD scored a try a in quick response to the WP Ryan Davids 2nd try, and that seemed to be the kick start that the side needed as they went on to add 9 more points through JT Jackson and win the game 22-19.
