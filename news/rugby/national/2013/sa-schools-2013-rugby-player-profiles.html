A 28 man squad was selected to represent South Africa U18 in the upcoming series against England, Wales, and France. The SARU national rugby weeks focus on the primary development of our rugby talent. Starting at the U13 level, followed by U16 Grant Khomo week that features an U16 high performance camp for excelling players.

The final step of the youth development would be the U18 Academy and Craven Weeks where players get the SA Academy team call up and the SA Schools rugby squad call up.

Failure to make any of these teams mentioned doesn’t mean that a player isn’t good enough: to quote SA U20 Baby Bok coach Dawie Theron

“I always believe that every day a new star can be born, so if the guys keep on working hard even if they don’t make the Craven Week side or SA Schools side they can still get to the top”

A great example of the quote above would be Bryan Habana who didn’t play in any national youth rugby weeks, but still achieved some of SA’s highest rugby accolades.

Rikus Bothma

Height 1.87m
Weight 102 kg
Team Western Province
School Paarl Gymnasium
Position 8th Man & Captain
Craven Week Points 20

The 8th man topped and outstanding Craven Week 2013 by being named the 2013 SA Schools rugby Captain that will face England, France, and Wales next month. The 8th man shone throughout the season for his school Paarl Gymnasium, as their 1st XV hasn't lost a game this season, playing 16, drawing 1 and winning 15. Rikus Bothma is one of the top 8th men in the country who possesses aggressive ball carrying and defensive skills. Rikus Bothma was a powerhouse for his Western Province team as he scored a try in every match he played in. 

Daniel du Plessis

Height 1.78m
Weight 107 kg
Team Western Province
School Paarl Boys
Position Hooker
Craven Week Points 0

The Paarl Boys High strong forward has made a big an impression this year with solid performances for his school as Boishaai have only lost four games out of 14 this season. The hooker performed during Craven Week for WP as they went on to win the unofficial final in 2013 against the Lions. Daniel is also highly capable of making strong runs and defends well against opposition attack.

The lock has become a familiar face in the SA Schools side as he makes his return to the 2013 squad. Daniel captained the KZN team at this year's Craven Week brings a lot of strength at breakdowns and to defence. Daniel du Preez never played Grant Khomo week as he jumped straight into U18 Craven Week in 2011 and has attended all to date.

The strong flanker makes his return to the 2013 team. Jean-Luc has played in national rugby weeks since 2008 and has played in three U18 Craven Weeks. Du Preez's physicality makes him a great wall of defence and a bulldozer on attack.

Ruan Kramer

Height 1.87m
Weight 117kg
Team Free State
School Grey College
Position Tight Head Prop
Craven Week Points 5

Ruan Kramer has been an integral part in the Free State provincial squads as he has played in the all youth national rugby weeks since the U13 level in 2008. Kramer formed a formidable pack alongside Andrew du Plessis and Ox Nche for the Free State this year. Kramer's hard work and consistency at the breakdowns earned him the 2012 SA Schools rugby selection.

Thabani Sanda Mtsi is a well built prop and carries a lot of strength at the breakdowns and during the run of play. Mtsi's strong running and solid defence make him an impact player for this year's SA Schools team.  The prop is also very quick on his feet, a hard runner, and has a mean hand off.

Ox Nche

Height 1.82m
Weight 105kg
Team Free State
School HTS Louis Botha
Position Loose Head Prop
Craven Week Points 5
The 2012 SA Schools player strengthened the Free State scrum and lineouts. Nche's effectiveness at the breakdown was evident from the high number of phases his team was playing. Nche topped his performance when he broke away from the EP 22 to strech over for a try carrying three defenders with him. Nche was awarded the man of the match award. - See more at: http://www.hssm.co.za/news/rugby/national/2013/11-craven-week-top-perfor...

The 2012 SA Schools player has become a household name in the Free State as Ox played a pivotal role for his team's forward pack this year. Nche played in the Grant Khomo week in 2011 and since then has played at the highest level of schoolboy rugby. Nche is very effective at the breakdown, is a strong ball carrier and is more than able to go over for his team when needed to.

Abongile Nonkontwana

Height 1.96m
Weight 108kg
Team Blue Bulls
School St Albans
Position Lock
Craven Week Points 0

The returning 2012 SA Schools player started in the Border region while he was attending Selborne College and played U13 Craven Week in 2008 and U16 Grant Khomo in 2011. Abongile carries a lot of strength both in defence and attack making him an asset for the Blue Bulls. The lock plays a solid game with the forward pack and can easily link up between his backline teammates during attacking play.

Position Flanker

This prop-turned-flanker has featured in 6 national youth rugby weeks since 2007. Rampeta possesses strength, strong ball carrying, and good support play for his forward pack.

JD's excellent lineout work is arguably unrivaled against his opponents.

Francois Steyn

Francois Steyn

Height 1.77m
Weight 95kg
Team Blue Bulls
School Affies
Position Hooker
Craven Week Points 0

Francois‘s stature and reach make him well-built to contest as a promising hooker. Francois has one of the best lineout throws in schoolboy rugby and he has no trouble executing. Steyn also carries a burst of pace with him and proves to be a surprisingly lethal runner especially against backline players. Steyn played his second U18 Craven Week this year and he shows signs of great future rugby potential.

PJ Toerien

Team Blue Bulls
School Garsfontein
Position Flanker
Craven Week Points 0

The Garsies flanker earned his way into the 2013 SA Schools team through hard work and an overall consistent display of a technically clinical flanker.

Jacques Vermeulen

Height 1.97m
Weight 104kg
Team Western Province
School Paarl Gim
Position Flanker
Craven Week Points 5

The strong flanker has played in national youth rugby weeks since the U13 level and his strength in defence and attack make him an immense threat to any opposition. The flanker is insane at the breakdowns as he is constantly contesting for the ball, often gaining possession for his team. Vermeulen has shown sublime form throughout the year for his school Paarl Gim who have had an unbeaten rugby season.

Conraad van Vuuren

Height 1.80m
Weight 107kg
Team Pumas
School Nelspruit HS
Position Tight Head Prop
Craven Week Points 5

The tight head prop performed well in this year's U18 Craven week to earn his 2013 selection after playing in the national rugby weeks since the U13 level. The strong prop utilizes his bursts of speed and his strength at the breakdowns and during scrums, qualifying him as an impact forward player.

Daniel du Plessis

Height 1.87m
Weight 93kg
Team Western Province
School Paul Roos Gymnasium
Position Centre
Craven Week Points 25

Pure class just about sums up Daniel du Plessis's performance during this year's Craven Week. He made his mark in the opening match as he ran in four tries for his team. Daniel's timely runs are capable of carving any defence lines. The centre is always hungry for ball and adds a great "X" factor to any side he is in.

Remu Malan

Remu Malan

Height 1.78m
Weight 78kg
Team South Western Districts
School Outeniqua
Position Scrumhalf
Craven Week Points 0

The versatile and nippy scrumhalf has proven time and time again his worth through consistent top drawer performances. The scrummie's agility and stepping abilities make him a handful against opposition. Remu can operate the blindside and feed his backline with precision almost always.

Warrick Gelant

Warrick Gelant

Height 1.81m
Weight 86 kg
Team South Western Districts
School Outeniqua
Position Centre/Utility Back
Craven Week Points DNP

Warrick Gelant's attacking display of rugby is unbelievable with his ability to turn a match around in a matter of minutes. Gelant has a quick step and is very strong in his runs and in defence. Warrick has scored 11 tries for his high school this year and hasn't stopped his good form throughout 2013. Although he was injured at this year's Craven Week, he is still very much a player to watch in the years to come.

Grant Hermanus

Height 1.79m
Weight 76 kg
Team Western Province
School Paarl Gym
Position Wing
Craven Week Points 45

The wing showed great maturity with the boot this year, scoring at total of 50 points for Western Province. Grant has all round kicking, passing, attacking, and defensive abilities, making him a solid part of the SA Schools team.

Dewald Human

Height 1.67m
Weight 65 kg
Team South Western Districts
School Outeniqua
Position Flyhalf
Craven Week Points 37

The prolific SWD flyhalf started his Craven Week campaign slowly, but soon blossomed into his potential as the competition progressed. Dewald has established himself as one of the most elusive, technical, and highly talented number 10's in the country. Although he has a lot of work to do with his boot, kicking might not be nessacary for him in this SA Schools team. Dewald is a top notch playmaker who can almost always tee up his backline for try scoring opportunities. Dewald might be smaller in size but there's no doubt he sure can tackle!

Malcolm Jaer

Height 1.7m
Weight 70 kg
Team Eastern Province
School Brandwag HS
Position Fullback
Craven Week Points 5

Malcolm Jaer played Grant Khomo in 2011, this year saw him achieve the EP Craven Week call up, and his performance there earned him the SA Schools call up. Malcolm is the only EP player to be named for the upcoming SA Schools rugby series. The fullback also plays flyhalf for his school, is equipped with a steady kicking boot, playmaking abilities, and runs well with ball in hand.

Jurie Linde

Jurie Linde

Height 1.85
Weight 86 kg
Team Blue Bulls
School Affies
Position Centre
Craven Week Points 10

The highly decorated 2012 SA Schools  player has featured in Grant Khomo week (2011), Craven Week (2012), and this year played in the U18 SA 7's team that won gold in Australia. The centre is lethal in attack and displays great vision for defence backline holes. Linde runs the 100m in 10.65 secs, adding to his attacking ability. Jurie Linde is a stern defender and a highly disciplined player.  

Justin Phillips

Height 1.81 m
Weight 76 kg
Team Blue Bulls
School Waterkloof
Position Scrumhalf
Craven Week Points 5

And exiciting scrumhalf with sharp instincts for the try line, Justin makes his return to the SA Schools team after a classy display in 2012. Phillips shows great future potential reminiscent of past springbok scrummies.

Brandon Thomson

Height 1.85 m
Weight 88 kg
Team Pumas
School Ermelo High
Position Flyhalf
Craven Week Points 40

The flyhalf scored a whopping 65 points in his 2013 Craven Week campaign, showing great kicking ability both for touch and for posts. Thomson featured in the Pumas 2012 Craven Week team and plays a good hand in leading his backline from the flyhalf position. He will definitely be a reliable member in the SA Schools team.

Duhan van der Merwe

Height 1.94 m
Weight 96 kg
Team SWD
School Outeniqua
Position Wing
Craven Week Points 15

A bit shy off the field but a force to be reckoned with on it, Duhan's height and strength give him a great advantage down the wing position. His runs are explosive and his ability to pick up speed make him tremendously lethal in attack. Van der Merwe has a great hand off and enjoys crash ball situations and is a hardworking, passionate player who's always hungry for the ball. Duhan also played in this year's U18 7's team that triumphed in Australia, and is a decorated player achieving 2012 SA Schools, 2011 Grant Khomo, and 2012 & 2013 Craven Week selections.

EW Viljoen

Height 1.92 m
Weight 92 kg
Team Free State
School Grey College
Position Fullback
Craven Week Points 10

The graceful fullback has played in all national rugby weeks since the U13 age group. EW was dubbed to be selected and he certainly proved the pundits correct. Viljoen shows a maturity for reading the game way beyond his years, at fullback he's an excellent defender, and he can kick for touch and posts well. EW also joins the backline in attack perfectly adding to his gameplay.

Leolin Zass

Height 1.84 m
Weight 82 kg
Team Boland
School Hermanus High
Position Centre
Craven Week Points 5

The sole selection from the Boland team, Zass shows good speed and defence. Zass provided an edge for Boland this year and worked hard to earn the 2013 SA Schools call up.
