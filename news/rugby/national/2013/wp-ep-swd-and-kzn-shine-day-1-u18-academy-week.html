Eastern Province vs Blue Falcons

Eastern Province's running style of play saw the boys from Port Elizabeth run in 10 tries. EP flanker Johan van Niekerk stood out from the onset scoring twice in quick succession. Flyhalf Darren Domingo coolly slotted 8 out of 10 try conversions, notching up 16 points for EP. The Blue Falcons couldn’t answer the EP forwards as JJ Killian and Robin Stevens got onto the score sheet.  EP’s Thabani Mgudu played a good tactical kicking game through accurate grubbers in the Blue Falcon 22. Blue Valke scored a well worked try at the very end of the second half, centre Reinhardt Stols dotting down.

Full Time: Eastern Province 66 - 5 Blue Falcons

Golden Lions vs SWD

The boys from Gauteng came out firing on all cylinders as SWD kicked off as the Lions applied early pressure and utilized their large forward pack. The match remained even, both teams trading tries, putting the match on a knife's edge. SWD centre JT Jackson displayed superb form as he kicked 18 points [4 conversions + 3 penalties].  The drama started in the 62nd minute as Stefan Fitzgerald took the Lions back into the lead when the game stood deadlocked, SWD’s Lenesteyo Nomdo scored and JT Jackson had to convert to put his team back on level terms 33 – 33, much to his agony JT Jackson missed his first conversion of the match, as everyone thought he would be made to roe for his miss - 31 – 33 to the Lions with 5 minutes till full time. The Lions restarted and kicked off deep into the SWD 22 and no one could have guessed what would happen next! As SWD fullback Ruan Barnard performed a strong run down the line, dodged defenders, and squeezed in at the corner for what was to be the match winning try. JT Jackson made no mistake and converted to end the game 38 – 33 to SWD.

Full Time: Golden Lions 33 - 38 SWD

Pumas vs Free State

Puma’s passion was enough to snatch the match out of Free State's hands. The physical game was to be won by the hungrier pack of forwards, which was the case in the second half as a try by prop Blessing Nxumalo spurred the Pumas into believing they could win. And so they did by hanging on to their four point lead with 5 minutes till full time defending relentlessly against the Free State forwards.

Full Time: Pumas 26 – 22 Free State
Match Breakdown here

Red Valke vs Western Province

WP’s Aiden Cupido scored a healthy 28 points from a possible 36 on the day, and Western Province as a whole tallied more tries than many of the matches that took place today. Red Valke had no response for the constant churning of phases from the WP team. WP Captain Dante van der Merwe proved to be key in WP's running style of rugby. Dante linked up with left wing Edwill van der Merwe as the two team mates scored a hat trick a piece. WP Forward Jaco Willemse also had a good game and rounded it off with two tries. WP will be a team to watch this 2013 U18 Academy Week after today’s performance.

Full Time: Western Province 93 – 0 Red Valke
Match Breakdown Here

Leopards CD vs Border CD

The Border team put up a good fight against the Leopards especially in the second half as they fought hard to concede one try. Leopards’ centre Pieter Roux scored 18 points for his team (2 tries and 4 conversions). Roux displayed daring individual skill as he ran from his team’s 22m beating 3 defenders, chipped and chased, and the bounce was in his favour as he scored a great try in the first half. Leopard’s scrumhalf Brady Williams scored a brace of tries to extend his team's lead.

Full Time: Leopards CD 38 – 0 Border CD
Match Breakdown Here

KZN vs Leopards

KZN’s ten try haul was hard fought in the end as the resilient Leopards team returned pressure in the last 5 minutes of the second half. KZN’s Chris Lines scored 12 points for the KZN team (2 tries and 1 conversion). Although the KZN team accumulated 54 points, the lack of a place kicker only saw the team convert 2/10 try conversions, with kicking duties being shared amongst Curtis Jonas, Chris Lines, and Kwazi Khanyile. Leopards’ John-Richard Larkins, and Johan van Niekerk each dotted down for their team with Crafford scoring in the last play of the match. KZN’s Brad Ellse had a great game at 13 points, and KZN forwards also came to the party as Waelon Hippolite, Jacques Conradi, and M Mazwi dotted down, claiming an opening victory over Leopards.

Full Time: KZN 54 – 20 Leopards
Match Breakdown Here

Limpopo Blue Bulls vs Border

The opening game of the  day on field B at Glenwood high saw the boys in blue from Limpopo take on a spirited Border side that was being cheered on by its country district side on the stand.  It was Limpopo who got off on the front foot with an early try from centre Vukosi Mabuza, which Diederick Oberholzer failed to convert. Border came back in the game minutes later with a try from Yavela Sigenu.

The 1st half finished with a narrow 12-10 last minute lead from Limpopo, who continued pilling on the pressure in the opening minute of the 2nd half, opening up the scoresheet with a try from Jino Moyo. It seemed all over for the Border side until fullback Michael Brink took the game in his own hands and single-handedly turned around Borders fortunes and led them to a 21-19 victory against the Limpopo Blue Bulls.

Full Time: Limpopo Blue Bulls 19 - 21 Border
Match Breakdown Here

Namibia vs SA LSEN

In what was a one sided points bonus for the visiting Namibia boys, they ran a complete riot against the SA LSEN side, which didn’t seem to have a response.

The SA LSEN appeared to have a promising 1st five minutes as they displayed the flair, speed, and skill of their backline which the Namibia defence managed to absorb. But once on defence the team seemed to crumble and the Namibians took advantage taking a 26-0 lead at the end of the first half through tries from centre Angelo Kotze, hooker and captain Gert Steenkamp, Jerome Beukes and Rudi Pieters.

Not much changed in the second half as the SA LSEN side continues to produce a disappointing attempt at defence, while the Namibians continued to exploit this and the game finished Namibia 52-0 LSEN.

Full Time: Namibia 52 - 0 SA LSEN
Match Breakdown Here

Griquas vs Griffons CD

Both teams started off well and tested out each other's mettle, and they seemed to be evenly matched.

It was Griffons CD who drew first blood with a penalty from standout player Juandre Kleynhans, but Griquas responded immediately with a penalty of their own from flyhalf Dirk van den Heever. It was back and forth points scoring that made for exciting rugby, but in the end it was Juandre’s incredible performance on the day which edged the game out for the Griffons as the won 39-25 against the Griquas.

Full Time: Griquas 25 - 39 Griffons CD
Match Breakdown Here

KZN B vs Griquas CD

It should have been a hard game for the KZN B side who had trained just once as the team two days before after they were quickly scrambled to replace the Kenya team that couldn’t make it due to rumoured finance issues. But the KZN B side surprised us all in attendance as the players grabbed the opportunity they were given and produced a cracker of a game.

It was the KZN side that got on to the scoresheet fist with a try from lighting fast winger Sam Matiwane, which seemed to open the floodgates.

The KZN team finished the 1st half with a 17-0 lead and were playing some beautiful and well-structured rugby for a side that just trained once before as a team. They started the second half in the same attacking manner they had finished the first half in and the Griquas side never seemed to threaten the opposition, seldom entering their 22. In the end KZN B went on to win 34-0 scoring 4 tries in the process.

Full Time: KZN B 34 - 0 Griquas CD
Match Breakdown Here

Blue Bulls vs Boland

The biggest upset of day 1 was Boland beating the Blue Bulls by a single point. The Boland boys got off to a flyer with an early penalty from man of the match flyhalf Ronald Brown putting them in the score sheet, and seemed to be in the driving seat throughout the whole first half. A try from centre Done’ Davids gave them a 10-3 lead at the end of the 1st half.

The Bulls came back a much stronger side in the 2nd half with flyhalf Thinus de Beer changing his boots for magical kicking boots, as it was through his pin point kicking that the Bulls made a huge comeback after being 13-3 down finishing the game 13-12 in a game were both flyhalves were outstanding.

Full Time: Blue Bulls 12 - 13 Boland
Match Breakdown Here

KZN CD vs Pumas CD

The last game of the day on field B saw the country district sides from KZN and Pumas take on each other, with the home side determined to prove themselves.

Man of the match Alex Booyse converted a penalty to open KZN CD account with Pumas responding soon after with a penalty of their own from Jesse du Toit. It was neck and neck for a while until a try from lock Blessing Nhlozi broke the dead lock for KZN, who then finished the 1st half with a 17-3 lead.

Puams CD came back in the second half a much more motivated side and got the opening try of the second half through Ray van der Lith, and lead through most of the second half until Alex Booysen and Blessing Nhlozi snatched the game away from the Pumas with two late tries from the pair to give KZN CD a 36-22 victory.

Full Time: KZN CD 36 - 22 Pumas CD
