Affies vs Grey College 1st XV Match Report 2013

Lungelo Mdladla
Tue, 14/05/2013 - 18:34

The much anticipated classic clash finally got underway as Grey College hosted Affies in a school rugby historic derby.

The tension was enormous as the Grey Bloem boys hit the field and the emotion on Heinrich Sander and Stephen Rautenbach’s face showed much of what the next 70 minutes really meant to the Grey Bloem team.

Kick off, and in the 1st minute Grey Bloem’s Stephen Rautenbach was the first to register points for his team as he calmly slotted a penalty over to put the hosts in the lead. At the restart a Grey College scrum saw a kick into Affies territory and 5 phases later Affies Jurie Linde performed his trademark run to carve the Grey defence before Grey Bloem’s EW Viljoen preformed a great cover tackle in his 22. Affie forwards charged to the ruck to keep the ball, Grey's defence was vulnerable on the blindside and Jurie tumbled over for the first Affies try, although the replay suggests Jurie might have dropped the ball as he was attempting to place the ball on the ground to score. The lack of a TMO resulted in the referee awarding the try. Ivan van Zyl stepped up to convert but the ball sailed wide much to his dismay, 5 – 3 to Affies in the 5th.

The restart saw a forward pass from Grey Bloem result in a Affies scrum, Affies kicked into the Grey College territory before the WP contracted 1.92 metre fullback EW Viljoen gracefully made a 40 metre run beating two tackles to put Grey College in a good try scoring position. Affies conceded a penalty and Grey Bloem’s blindside flanker, Captain Marius Louw, opted for the three points and outside centre Stephen Rautenbach delivered. 6 – 5 to Grey in the 12th minute.

In the 20th  Grey College were judged to be offside and Affies Ivan van Zyl stepped upped to convert confidently. 8 – 6 to Affies. The hosts reacted to the 3 point deficit quickly as EW Viljoen broke the Affies defence line, offloaded to DP du Plessis who passed to Henry Immelman and the left wing completed the move going over for Grey’s first try of the match. Rautenbach missed the conversion leaving the score 11 – 8 to Grey College in the 22nd minute.

At the restart Grey College kicked the ball straight back into Affies territory resulting in a lineout. Affies’s RG Snyman was on top form as he jumped high to collect Francois Steyn’s throws, and a 5 metre maul from Affies saw them build 8 phases before Thys van Wyk went over to score Affies' second try of the match. Ivan didn’t hesitate as he converted well to give Affies a slim 15 – 11 lead.

Grey Bloem kicked the ball out on the full at the restart and a 50 metre scrum was awarded to Affies. Affies gained some ground but kicked back into Grey Bloem territory where Grey Bloem built good phase play and Affies conceded a penalty due to hands in the ruck, Rautenbach coolly slotted. 15 – 14 to Affies in the 33rd minute.

The restart saw an error from the Grey Bloem’s no. 4 who dropped the ball allowing Affies hooker Francois Steyn to run over the try line untouched to score Affies third of the game. Ivan calmly converted over to take Affies to a 22 – 14 lead at the half time whistle.

In the second half Grey College started well as they carried 6 phases from their 22 to the Affies 22 but were unfortunate to concede a penalty that Affies cleared. The second half point scoring proceedings were kicked off by Affies skipper Ivan van Zyl as he scored 3 more for his team after Grey conceded a penalty, stretching  their lead to 25 – 14. Grey returned the favour as Affies conceded a penalty and Rautenbach’s conversion meant Grey Bloem trailed 25 – 17. Grey College were on the hunt for needed points and were agonisingly close to scoring their first try of the second half but were instead held up on the try line by an impressive Affies defence. In the 40th minute Affie skipper Ivan van Zyl was finally awarded with a try after Francois Steyn charged down a kick in the Grey College 22 that Ivan picked up to dot down much to his jubilation. His successful conversion saw Affies lead 32 – 17. Seven minutes later centre Jurie Linde scored his second try of the game and the missed conversion left the derby at 37 – 17 to Affies.

The sense of urgency from Grey Bloem was more than ever at this point, needing 21 points to win the game with 20 minutes to play. Grey’s rolling maul in the 60th minute was held up over the try line, and an offside offence from Affies played to Grey’s advantage, Grey Bloem opted for the scrum and Andrew du Plessis crashed over in the 62nd minute. Rautenbach converted to bring the hosts ever so close at 24 – 37.

The final 13 minutes saw what was to be the last try of the game as Grey College’s Callie Janse van Vuuren rumbled over for a try in the 70th minute. Rautenbach performed a quick drop goal conversion, to put his team a converted try away from a hard fought victory at 31 – 37.  

Grey Bloem were unfortunate to knock the ball on and Affies were awarded a scrum and Affies conveniently slowed the ball down at the breakdown and an error from Grey Bloem was followed by the sound of the final whistle.
