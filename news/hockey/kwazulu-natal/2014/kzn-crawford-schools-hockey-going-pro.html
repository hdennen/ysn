KZN Crawford Schools Hockey Going Pro

By: 
Lungelo Mdladla
Mon, 05/05/2014 - 17:30

KZN Crawford Schools are taking a new direction in terms of their hockey. Primarily Crawford La Lucia as they bring on board well renowned KZN hockey Coach Justin Collins. Collins' approach to hockey saw a successful Glenwood 1st XI team and he now takes on the broader challenge at Crawford. Crawford schools will now turn over a new leaf as their focus on hockey is expanding from the juniors all the way up to the seniors.

The current Crawford Schools hockey vision is to develop a player's basic skills and push the limits of players. In due time Crawford will be sure to have provincial and national representatives for both boys and girls.`Crawford will also run a hockey goal keeping academy run by Dave Staniforth former, SA goalkeeper and current Indian goal keeping coach, that will be used to help hockey goal keepers around the province on a weekly basis.

We chat to Crawford's new KZN Hockey Coordinator Justin Collins and he shares his views on his new task at KZN Crawford schools and Crawford La Lucia. 

Achievements

Provincial Coaching - Hockey

  • Natal U21A Ladies Indoor 2011 – 2012 [Winners of the IPT for both years]
  • Natal U21A Ladies Outdoor 2011 - 2012
  • Natal Senior Ladies Selector 2011
  • Natal U18 Indoor Boys Convener of Selectors 2012
  • Natal U21A Men Coach (Indoor and Outdoor) 2013, 2014
  • Natal U18A Boys Coach (Outdoor) 2013, 2014

Club Coaching - Hockey

  • Collegians Ladies 1st side 2009 - 2011
  • Durban North Youth Hockey 2010 - 2011
  • Head of Durban North Mini Hockey 2010 – 2011
  • Munies Hockey Club 1st Team Men 2013, 2014
  • Head of Old Crawfordian's mini hockey 2014

School Coaching

  • Glenwood High School U14A Hockey 2006 - 2007
  • Glenwood High School U16A Hockey 2008 - 2009
  • Glenwood High School Hockey Academy 2009 - 2012
  • Chelsea Preparatory 1st Team Hockey - Boys 2010 - 2013
  • Durban Girls College Junior and Senior Primary Hockey 2009 - 2011
  • Crawford North Coast 1st Team Hockey Girls and Boys 2010 - 2011
  • Glenwood High School 1st Team Hockey Coach and Coaching Co-Coordinator 2011 – 2013 
  • Crawford schools Natal hockey co-coordinator 2014 

Personal Sporting Achievements

  • UKZN 1st Team Hockey 2006, 2007
  • Made the u/21 Mynahs indoor hockey side - April 2006
  • U21A Raiders Team outdoor 2006
  • KZN hockey umpire 2006
  • U21A Raiders team indoor 2007
  • U21A Raiders team outdoor 2007
  • Mynah’s men’s side 2007, 2008, 2009
  • Mynah’s men’s indoor side: 2008, 2009, 2010, 2014
  • Raiders Men’s Outdoor 2010 – 2012
  • Raiders Men’s Indoor 2012
  • Durban North 1st side indoor 2008 - 2012
  • Durban North 1st side Outdoor 2008 – 2012
  • Munies Hockey Team (Player/Coach) 2013
  • Mike Madsen xi 2012 – 2014

You have spent quite a number of years in the KZN hockey scene, what advantages and learning experience do you want to bring to the table at Crawford schools?
Justin Collins: I think it has helped a lot that I have had exposure to provincial and national courses in order to gain knowledge of vital information to grow a system such as Crawford. I had the task of bringing the Glenwood system into place back in 2006 and that wasn’t a walk in the park until we saw the growth and development which can be seen there at present, which gives me the confidence to work with the talent at a school like Crawford. The most important thing is to sort out the coaches and get the structures right so we can get the same system played from the juniors all the way through to our seniors.

How do you feel that KZN Crawford schools are second tier hockey playing schools, and what strategies do you plan on implementing in order for Crawford La Lucia to raise their standards on a provincial level?
Justin Collins: I don’t feel it's correct in saying that it’s a second tier school as our girls have been a part of the “A” league for the past couple of years where they having been competing with the likes of St Mary’s and Durban Girls College. In fact our 1st team boys beat DHS 1st XI in a recent match and they are classified as an ‘A league’ school.  At our junior levels of our boys we have a strong bunch coming through so that is so important in the development of the hockey programme. My main aim is to get as much knowledge to the kids at an u14 and younger level at our school so we can be developed and get more Natal representatives on board. Our astro is going to aid with this and the most important thing is to keep challenging the kids. Having our own world class facility is also going to bode well for our kids. With having the facility we are also instilling an elite session to focus and develop players skills as well as goal keeper session to develop our all round game. 

You Coached a very successful Glenwood 1st XI that achieved quite a lot, can you tell us more about your season with the team and on why you departed from Glenwood?
Justin Collins: Yes it was a very successful 3 years with the Glenwood boys 1st XI Hockey but that didn’t happen overnight. It was a long process of developing the correct kids at junior levels which can be seen now. I think the most important key to success to the Glenwood side was that we kept pushing the boys and developed skills that needed to be looked after but most importantly to get a talented bunch of individuals to play as a team and realize that they need to work for each other. It was a difficult decision to make to leave the Glenwood team as this could be one of the strongest teams in the country this year but at the end of the day I felt my time was up at Glenwood and I needed to move on and share my knowledge with Crawford. I want to thank Glenwood for the last 8 years and for helping me to get to the place that I am now.  

What obstacles and challenges do you feel lie ahead of you in this new position?
Justin Collins: I feel that there aren’t going to be many challenges ahead of me just long hours and hard work to get the structures up and running.  My biggest focus is to have kids attending and get the participation up. I feel if a kid can hold a hockey stick they can develop their skills and compete at the highest level. We are going to have an internal High Performance clinic as part of the programme as well as professional goal keeping clinics hosted on a weekly basis and the Old Crawfordian's Mini Hockey Academy which will develop our juniors from the U6's to 1st XI both boys and girls.

Can you tell us a bit more about the Old Crawfordian's Club?
Justin Collins: Old Crawfordian's is an ‘Open Club’ meaning that anyone can join and we already have students signed up from schools all over the greater Durban area. Old Crawfordian's Mini Hockey Academy will cater for boys and girls from the ages of 5 to 13. At the Old Crawfordian's Mini Hockey Academy we aim to develop and equip our future stars with the necessary skills and knowledge to develop and further their hockey. The academy is focused on providing players with the appropriate skills according to their age group, as well as teaching them the correct ethics and attitude needed for hockey, sport and life in general.

Can you tell us a bit more about the new state of the art hockey programme?
Justin Collins: I wouldn’t say that I have the state of the art hockey programme, rather I have a goal and a direction I want to go. If my vision is clear and focused by implementing my structures I am hoping that naturally we will go forward. This isn’t going to happen overnight even though we have seen a growth in the players already, but rather within the next four or five years once our development of our younger players starts to flourish. Hockey is not a complimented game but a very technical and tactical game so if we sort out the finer details the programme will take off.

What motto do you live by that you’d like to see displayed by your athletes from the juniors all the way up to the seniors?
Justin Collins: My main motto in life, and has been since I was a little boy, is to never give up until you have achieved what you wanted to achieve. “Never give up” is my life motto and I feel that this is going to be a key to success at Crawford schools.

KZN Hockey has had its great years in the past and has always provided a lot of SA school players, why do you think that is and how do you think we can keep competing with the strong WP, Border, and GP sides?
Justin Collins: I feel we contribute well because we have a good core of schools that provide top level programmes and we look to develop players all round games and not focus on individuals. The key to success at various levels at KZN coastal levels has come from coaches putting in the hard yards to develop team work alongside with all the technical areas in the game. I think it would be fair to congratulate all the coaches in KZN setups as well as schools for implementing the correct structures for the province as a whole to do well in all the age groups.

YSN thanks Justin Collins for his time and all the best in his new position!
