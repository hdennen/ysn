Northwood Survive Glenwood Comeback

By: 
Lungelo Mdladla
Sazi Luke
Mon, 17/09/2012 - 21:53
Matthew De Sousa
Dayne Naidoo
Tanner Bottomley
John McAlery
Dale van der Merwe

The first semi-final of the Westville Indoor Hockey Tournament kicked off with Glenwood playing against Northwood. Both teams have shown good form throughout the tournament and with just one game away from the final, both teams brought their A game.

Glenwood started the better of the two teams with the majority of early possession. They pinned Northwood back, starving them of the ball and forcing them to play the counter attack. The Northwood defence stood firm to halt the Glenwood attack. An opportunity finally came for Northwood on the penalty spot, and they made no mistake taking the lead through number 6 John McAlery.

Northwood gained momentum with more possession following the goal and began increasingly testing the Glenwood keeper. Glenwood failed to capitalise on scoring opportunities as they fired 5 shots at the goal with none ending up in the back of the net. Northwood continued to gain attacking confidence as the half went on taking more shots at the keeper. The effort soon paid off as they managed another goal by Dale Van Der Merwe through a counter attack from the back. The Glenwood keeper was made to work hard as Northwood fired a total of 9 shots for the half. The half ended 2-0 to Northwood with both keepers making great saves against maintained attacks.

Glenwood began the second half with the same style as the first, but proved more clinical in front of goal this time around. Their first goal came from a penalty corner that was brilliantly drag flicked by Dayne Naidoo into the net. Glenwood then went on to get a second penalty corner, which they converted through Tanner Bottomley.

Scores were now level 2-2 with Northwood answerless to the two quick Glenwood goals. Things did not get better for the Northwood boys as Glenwood went ahead for the first time in the game though open play as Tanner Bottomley got his brace of the day. The comeback seemed secure for Glenwood but a second goal from Dale Van der Merwe quickly levelled the scores 3 all.

Both sides upped their attacks as they took more shots at goal forcing their keepers into more critical saves. Glenwood soon had a total of 7 shots to Northwood’s 6. Northwood broke the deadlock by scoring through Matthew De Sousa to retake the lead from Glenwood. After a valiant comeback, Glenwood looked on as De Sousa scored another goal to collect his brace. In a last ditch effort Glenwood pulled their keeper for the extra man in outfield, but the effort went unrewarded as the final whistle put Northwood victorious at 5-3.

Coaches take:

Glenwood coach, Justin Collins

HSSM: Disappointing result for you guys after you made what seemed to be a great result.

Justin Collins: "I mean we had a young side, so we are trying to build it for next year too.. It was a little bit disappointing. They scored two quick goals after we had gone up in the lead. So it was a little bit disappointing."

HSSM: So what went wrong for you today?

Justin Collins: "I don't know.. I think we weren't as consistent as we have been, and it's been a little bit disappointing."

 

Northwood coach, Darryn Gallagher

HSSM: Your team left a bit too late to establish its dominance, why was this the case?

Darryn Gallagher: "Well I think the first half we dominated properly, but i think Glenwood in the 2nd half upped the intensity and stepped really hard on us which made the difference, and my guys got flustered as a result. But once we got our starting six guys back on, we got the control back."
