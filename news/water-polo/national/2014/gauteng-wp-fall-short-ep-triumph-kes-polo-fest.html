Gauteng & WP Fall Short As EP Triumph At The KES Polo Fest

Lungelo Mdladla
Wed, 12/03/2014 - 08:41

The rise of EP water polo schools saw Grey PE and St Andrews College finish 1st and 2nd whereas in 2012 St Andrews College only managed to finish 3rd in the plate section while Grey High weren’t even invited.

Grey High's trail up the water polo scene was first noticed in their SACS tournament championship victory. In 2013 St Andrews clinched first place in the plate section at the KES water polo tournament while Grey High finished 6th in the Cup section.

During all of this, the usual suspects Clifton, St Stithians, Reddam, and St Johns were dominating the water polo scene much to the dismay of the EP schools.

Gauteng, KZN, and WP have always had stronger school teams, and players from these three provinces always jam packed the SA Schools teams.

2012 saw no EP player in the KES tournament team and in 2013 Cameron Johnson was the only player to make the cut. This year we see three Grey boys, and two boys from St Andrews. But the cherry on top has to be Grey PE’s and SA Schools 2013 and 2014 player Jason Evezard being named player of the tournament.

The tables are turning but all in the name of the improvement of SA’s national standard of water polo. While I wish we could have seen Clifton and Reddam participate in this year’s tournament, it was a spectacle nonetheless.

I was disappointed about how the Gauteng schools did as they are usually so dominant

We chat to St Andrews’s and EP U18 water polo Coach Graeme Lucas on what this could mean for the future of EP polo and what he feels St Andrews could have done to win. We also chat to Grey PE’s manager Anneliese van Niekerk on her team’s winning performance.

It's good to see EP schools dominating this year, why do you think that is and what would you say is changing within the school's water polo?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: It is very exciting for the Eastern Cape. I think the reason for it is the talent pool at the moment. The current players (grade 11 & 12s) are a very talented bunch of Polo players. They have always dominated since U13 right the way through.

From other schools: Jordan Chait and Rumbelow from SACS. Connor Timm (goal keeper), Jason Everzard, Jeremy Ward and Greg Aubin from Grey. Nicholas van der Elst from Saints. This group really stood out for me in the games they played.

Which players would you say stood out for you personally at the tournament?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: From my own team I would say Joey Slack my goal keeper had a fantastic tournament, Mat Andrews my Captain, Mat Stopforth and Max Kieser. Not to mention my Centre back Tim Treagus who was outstanding on defence. From other schools: Jordan Chait and Rumbelow from SACS. Connor Timm (goal keeper), Jason Everzard, Jeremy Ward and Greg Aubin from Grey. Nicholas van der Elst from Saints. This group really stood out for me in the games they played.

Can you describe what it took for you guys to get to the final?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: It took concentration and focus. I always maintain that mental strength and focus is key to any success and my players had that. They played smart calculated polo and were patient and executed well against all their opposition. I also think our tour to Cape Town the week before was great preparation for the tournament. I believe my players played so well as a team and never gave up no matter what and they are a happy group of young men that play for each other. That is how we got there.

You guys were unlucky to narrowly go down by 10 - 8, can you describe how the final was and how did you feel the guys played?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: The final was your typical final. Nothing separated the sides in the beginning. We were 1-1 at the end of the 1st quarter I think, and then 3-3 by half time. However, a lapse in concentration and focus in the 3rd quarter let us down. We lost that section 4-1 and even though we came back hard in the 4th it was too little too late. We won the last chukka 4-3.

I think the guys played outstanding polo and yes we lost our focus in key moments, but that is life and Grey were the better team on the day.

I don't like to make a big thing on tournament sides, especially when selected by one person. I would have liked to see more EP players in that team due to our dominance

You have two guys who were selected for the tournament team: Max and Matt, how do you think they played throughout the tournament?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: These two were outstanding for the College team. Max and Matt have been our top goal scorers all season and it’s only fitting that they are rewarded for it. I don't like to make a big thing on tournament sides, especially when selected by one person. I would have liked to see more EP players in that team due to our dominance at the tournament. Anyway, I am very happy for those two players they are definitely deserving of their selection.

If there was something you feel you guys should have done differently what would it be?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: I believe we did all we could on the day and Grey just were better. I do believe if we had shot the ball off the water more it may have worked in our favour as Connor Timm (Grey Goalie) is very strong on the straight shots and one needs to bounce the ball in order to test him. However, I am very pleased with our performance as it is the 1st time a St. Andrew's side has made it past the quarter finals.

I would have really liked to see how Clifton and Reddam would have fared this year. I really believe the Clifton side is the team to beat and it is the only side we have not beaten.

How was the general competition this year and how do you see the rest of the season going with SAC?
Graeme Lucas-Bull: The competition was fierce as always. I was disappointed about how the Gauteng schools did as they are usually so dominant, however maybe the rest of the country is just stronger. I would have really liked to see how Clifton and Reddam would have fared this year. I really believe the Clifton side is the team to beat and it is the only side we have not beaten.

The season is now over and we are preparing for provincial competition in March which is going to be very exciting. I will be coaching the EP U18 side which I am very excited about. I am looking forward to the end of the year and travelling to CT to participate in the SACS tournament. I really believe my team will be right up there with the tops sides.

Annelise van Neikerk Interview:

Well done on your success, What factors do you think contributed to your teams success?
Anneliese van Niekerk:  We have a group of players that are very talented this year and a new coach Paul Mackenzie. He prepared them well by adapting his game to play at altitude.

How did your players feel throughout the tournament and how did you keep them positive till the final?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk: The players got more motivated when they reached the quarter finals seeing that Grey never reached a position higher than QF before. It motivated them to break the curse. They had the mental strength to persevere right to the end of the final.  

Can you describe how the final was against St Andrews?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk: It was a very even game in which St Andrews kept fighting back. We had attack upon attack with St Andrews never giving up. This made the final very exciting till the last minute. Grey maintained their cool and showed mental strength throughout.

Which players stood out for you personally?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk: A very difficult task because everyone worked very hard throughout the tournament. The starting seven players were given enough time during the semi-final to catch their breath. This indicates a strong bench and therefore enough depth in the team.

the team with the best bench will most probably win the tournament.

Where there any moments of doubt when you felt you weren't going to win the tournament and why?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk:  We came to KES to try and make the quarter-finals. We didn’t expect to make it further than semi’s at the beginning. The coach said from the beginning that the team with the best bench will most probably win the tournament. I think we had a superb starting seven and a bench who came into the pool with confidence.

How was your overall experience at KES?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk: I think it is a very good tournament, showcasing the best water polo teams in the country. It was very well run and organised. The length of the games also helped to show the strength and character of the players.

And lastly can you describe Jason Evezards performance at the tournament?
Anneliese E. van Niekerk: He is a very talented all round pupil who pushes himself very hard. He is also a very humble person with a very likeable personality. He pushes himself continuously and works very long and hard towards his goals. Another person who I thought did extremely well was the captain Jeremy Ward.
